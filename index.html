📱 The Trigger
Your iPhone Shortcut automation watches for incoming texts matching keywords like "referral," "I got a patient for you," "sending someone your way" — or any text from specific sender contacts you configure. Runs silently in the background. Your referral sources never know it's there.
🧠 The AI Brain
Claude receives the full raw text and asks: is this a real patient referral or just a general conversation about referrals? It looks for patient-specific signals — a phone number, a name, a complaint, an email. If any are found, it flags it as a real referral and the automation continues. It then restructures the messy text into clean, standardized fields.
📍 Clinic Matching
If a zip code or area code is provided, the AI locates the 2 nearest clinics. If no location info is given, it defaults to showing upcoming available slots at the most common/accessible clinics in your network.
⚡ Simultaneous Actions
Everything fires at once: Google Sheet gets the structured data, team gets the WhatsApp blast, patient gets a welcome email (if email was included), and the referral source gets a warm, human-sounding thank-you reply within 30 minutes.
⏰ The Safety Net
Twice a day (9 AM + 4 PM), the system checks for any referrals that haven't been called within their SLA window and sends an alert to the whole team. Nothing falls through the cracks.
The key idea: From the moment someone texts you a referral to the moment your team member has the patient on the phone with appointment options — zero manual work from you. The system handles parsing, organizing, prioritizing, notifying, and nagging.

How the iPhone Shortcut Trigger Works

The Shortcut automation lives on your iPhone and fires automatically when you receive a text that matches either of these conditions:

A

Keyword Match

Any incoming text containing trigger words: "referral," "I got a patient for you," "sending someone your way," "new patient," or other phrases you configure. You can add, remove, or adjust keywords anytime.

B

Specific Sender

Any text from contacts you've designated as referral sources — regardless of what the message says. If Dr. Ramirez is in your sender list, every text from him gets forwarded to the AI for analysis.

You can use either mode or both at once. The AI handles the second layer of filtering — even if the Shortcut triggers on a casual text from Dr. Ramirez about golf, the AI will recognize it's not a real referral and ignore it. No false alarms.

02 The Workflow — Real Examples

Example 1 — Detailed Referral

Dr. Ramirez texts you at 2:17 PM on a Tuesday:

Dr. Ramirez
Hey got a referral for you. Maria Garcia 786-555-1234 maria.garcia@email.com car accident last Thursday, neck and back pain. She lives in Doral area 33178. Cervical strain, lumbar radiculopathy. Attorney is Smith & Associates. She needs to be seen ASAP.

Here's what happens in the next 8 seconds:

Second 1-2 — iPhone

📱 Shortcut fires silently

Your phone detects "referral" in the message and sends the entire raw text to your Google Apps Script webhook. You don't see or do anything.

Second 3-5 — AI Analysis

🧠 Claude reads the full message

Step 1 — Real referral? Claude scans for patient-specific details. Finds a phone number, name, email, complaint, diagnosis — clearly a real referral. Automation continues.

Step 2 — Extract & restructure: Pulls out all the messy details and organizes into standardized fields: Maria Garcia, (786) 555-1234, maria.garcia@email.com, Car Accident, cervical strain + lumbar radiculopathy, 33178, attorney Smith & Associates.

Step 3 — Flag gaps: Insurance not mentioned — flagged as missing.

Step 4 — Priority: 🔴 T1 URGENT — recent MVA + attorney + "ASAP".

Step 5 — Clinics: Area code 786 + zip 33178 → locates 2 nearest clinics: Doral (2.1 mi), Hialeah (4.8 mi). Pulls available slots in next 48 hours.

Second 6-7 — Everything fires

📊💬📧 Simultaneous actions

Google Sheet: New row with all structured data + checkboxes. REF-0042.

WhatsApp blast: Full referral details + nearest clinics + appointment slots → sent to entire team.

Welcome email: Sent to maria.garcia@email.com with practice info, booking link, and what to bring.

Within 30 minutes — Auto-reply

↩️ Thank-you text to Dr. Ramirez

A warm, human-sounding reply goes back to the referral source — not instant (which would feel robotic), but within 30 minutes to feel natural: "Hey Dr. Ramirez — got it, Maria Garcia is in our system. Team will be reaching out to her shortly. Thanks for thinking of us, really appreciate it!"

Then your team member calls. Sarah sees the WhatsApp, opens the sheet, sees Maria is 🔴 Urgent with 3 appointment options. She calls: "Hi Maria, we have openings at our Doral location — Tuesday at 2 PM, Wednesday at 10:30 AM, or Thursday at 3 PM." Maria picks one. Sarah checks two boxes. Done.

04 The Tier System

The AI auto-assigns priority from the referral text. Your team never thinks about tiers — they see the color and the deadline.

Tier Call Within What Triggers It
🔴 T1
URGENT		 1 hour Recent accident (<7 days), ER discharge, attorney involved, "ASAP"/"urgent", patient in acute pain
🟠 T2
PRIORITY		 4 hours Accident within 30 days, has imaging/diagnosis, complete info, insurance verified
🟡 T3
STANDARD		 24 hours Chronic pain, no time pressure, vague complaint, older injury
⚪ T4
NEEDS INFO		 24 hours
(flags you)		 Missing phone number, can't identify patient, referral too vague to act on
Why this matters for pain management: Recent MVA patients are the highest-value referrals. They need care fast — if your team waits, those patients find another practice or the attorney sends them elsewhere. Attorney involvement always means T1 because that patient is being referred to multiple practices. First to call wins.

Why Tiering Directly Generates More Revenue

Without a tier system, every referral is treated the same. Your team works through them in whatever order they feel like — or worse, picks the easy ones first. The problem: the referrals worth the most money are the ones most sensitive to time.

💰 The Math on MVA + Attorney Cases

A single car accident patient with an attorney can generate $5,000 — $50,000+ in treatment revenue (imaging, injections, physical therapy, follow-ups over 6-18 months). These cases are the financial backbone of a PI-focused pain management practice. But if your team calls 3 hours late, that patient is already booked at the practice down the street.

⏱️ The Competitive Window

Attorneys and chiropractors typically refer to 2-3 practices simultaneously. The first practice that calls, sounds organized, and offers a convenient appointment wins the case. It's not about being the best doctor — it's about being the fastest to respond. A 1-hour SLA on T1 makes you that practice.

📊 Revenue per Tier (Typical Ranges)

🔴 T1 (MVA + Attorney): $10K-$50K/case
🟠 T2 (MVA, no attorney yet): $5K-$20K/case
🟡 T3 (Chronic/standard): $2K-$8K/case
Without tiering, your team might call the $3K chronic pain patient first while the $25K MVA+attorney case sits for 4 hours and walks.

🔄 The Referral Source Flywheel

Fast response doesn't just win one patient — it wins the relationship. An attorney who sends you a patient and sees that you called within 45 minutes, booked same-week, and sent a confirmation? That attorney sends you every case from now on. One attorney sending 20 cases/year at $15K average = $300K/year in revenue from a single relationship, protected by a 1-hour SLA.

Bottom line: Tiering isn't just organization — it's a revenue prioritization engine. It ensures your team always works the highest-dollar referrals first, responds within the competitive window that wins cases, and builds the trust with referral sources that keeps the pipeline flowing. One T1 referral saved from going stale pays for this entire system for years.

05 What "Stale" Means

A stale referral is one where the patient hasn't been called within the expected timeframe for their priority tier.

Stale = lost revenue. Every stale referral is a patient getting colder by the hour. If your team doesn't call within the window, that patient books with another practice, their attorney moves them elsewhere, or they give up on getting care entirely.

The system makes stale referrals impossible to ignore through three layers:

1

Real-time embedding

Every new referral notification includes a count of stale referrals at the bottom. You can't see a new one without seeing the ones being ignored.

2

9 AM heartbeat

Stale referrals listed by name, phone number, and time overdue. Public accountability — entire team sees the same list.

3

4 PM heartbeat

Last chance before end of day. If something went stale during business hours, this catches it.

4

Owner escalation

If a T1 sits 2× past SLA, YOU get a direct personal alert separate from the group. Something is wrong.

The system eliminates "I didn't see it" and "I thought someone else was handling it." Every message is timestamped, every SLA is explicit, and the heartbeat checks make avoidance impossible.

06 The AI — Behind the Scenes

Claude (the AI) is the engine that turns messy text messages into clean, actionable data. Here's what it handles:

🔍

Referral Detection

"Hey bring referral forms to the conference" is NOT a referral. The AI reads context and filters junk. No false alarms.

📋

Data Extraction

Handles every format: formal referral letters, casual texts ("got a pt for u"), voice-to-text garble. Extracts name, phone, email, complaint, diagnosis, zip.

🎯

Priority Classification

"Last week" → recent MVA → 🔴 T1. "Attorney involved" → 🔴 T1. "Chronic for months" → 🟡 T3. Automatic.

⚠️

Missing Info Flags

Knows what a complete referral looks like. No phone number? No complaint? Flags exactly what's missing so the team knows before they call.

💳

Insurance Detection

Picks up on "GEICO," "Medicare," "workers comp," etc. Saves the team a question on the phone.

🛡️

Graceful Failure

If the AI can't parse a message, it logs it anyway as "NEEDS REVIEW" with the raw text. Nothing is ever lost.

07 Auto-Reply to Referral Sources

Within 30 minutes of processing a referral, the system sends a warm, natural-sounding thank-you back to the person who sent it. The delay is intentional — an instant reply feels robotic, but a reply within half an hour feels like a real person who checked their messages:

Auto-reply to Dr. Ramirez (sent ~25 min later)
Hey Dr. Ramirez — got it, Maria Garcia is in our system. Team will be reaching out to her shortly. Thanks for thinking of us, really appreciate it! 🙏

Why this matters:

  • Feels human, not automated. The 30-minute window and casual tone make it feel like you personally saw the text and responded — not a bot firing back in 0.3 seconds.
  • Professionalism. The source knows their referral was received and is being handled. Most practices never close this loop.
  • Trust = more referrals. When a referring doctor sees fast, reliable follow-through, they send more patients your way. This is a relationship builder.
  • Closes the loop. Without this, referring doctors wonder if their text went into a void. That uncertainty makes them less likely to refer again.
